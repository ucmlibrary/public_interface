Terms of Use and Copyright Information

About Calisphere

This site is managed by the California Digital Library. The content you find on this website, which includes both collection guides and digital items such as images, texts, and sound recordings, have been made publicly available by their owning institutions for research, teaching, and private study.

What you can do with the digital items

  • Using: Many items on the site are protected by copyright, but you may be able to use them for educational, research, or other purposes. You—not the California Digital Library or the item’s contributing institution—are responsible for determining whether your use is permitted by law. You can learn more about what uses are allowed by law at the American Library Association’s Copyright Advisory Network, which has tools to help explain copyright guidelines for instructors and fair use.
  • Linking: Anyone is welcome to view and link to any page of this site without further permission. This includes, for example, including a link to a particular page within this site from a syllabus or from your own website.
  • Citing: When citing or using material from the site, please provide information about the item so that others may find more information about the original. Include the name of the Contributing Institution, a link to the item, and title and date information when available.

What you can do with item information

Metadata about the digital items (the factual information describing the items) can be freely shared and reused.

  • All metadata records provided by Calisphere to the Digital Public Library of America (DPLA) are available under a Creative Commons Public Domain Dedication (CC0). Users are thus free to harvest, collect, modify and/or otherwise use these metadata records. Learn more about DPLA's policy for metadata records.

Where to go for more information

Calisphere contains a wide variety of items from many different institutions. Some items are in the public domain and may be freely used by anyone; for some items the contributing institution is the copyright holder and may grant permission for certain uses; at other times the copyright owner is a third party or is unknown and users will need to conduct their own analysis and may need to contact a copyright holder directly. To learn more about an item, users can consult the item level information and contact the contributing institution by clicking on the “contact owner” button or visiting their institution page for phone and email information.

If you have more information about something on the site

The nature of historical, archival, and/or primary source archival and manuscript collections and other primary source collections often can makes it difficult to determine details about the items that appear on this site. The California Digital Library and the contributing institution provide as much information as possible available, in the metadata associated with digital images, texts, and audio and video recordings, in accordance with commonly-accepted community standards and best practices. However, due to the limited resources available for investigating individual items and describing them fully, records may lack accurate and/or complete information about facts such as item’s exact age, persons or places depicted, creator, or copyright status.

If you have information about an item on this site that is not represented in the item’s metadata or would like to help correct an error, please contact the contributing institution by clicking on the “contact owner” button or visiting their institution page for phone and email information.

Join us!

Calisphere aggregates digital content and metadata from established collecting institutions in California. If you represent such an organization and are interested in contributing digital content to the site, please contact us.
